## Recent Projects

<div class="project-card">
  <div class="project-info">
    <h3>Social Robots</h3>
    <p>The goal of this project is to design robots that interact with each other, with animals, and with humans. Rather than programming fixed outcomes, this project harnesses <b>emergence</b> as a generative principle, engineering local interaction rules that give rise to <i>interesting, remarkable, and unusual</i> (Olkowski, 2011) behaviors that transcend the predictable. </p>
  </div>

<div class="project-card">
  <div class="project-info">
    <h3>Collective Dynamics</h3>
    <p>How do global behaviors arise from local interactions? We approach this question developing frameworks that bridge individual robot rules and population-level phenomena. This work spans thermodynamic models of active matter, large language models (LLMs) as high-level swarm controllers, and mean-field approaches to derive the emergent statistical mechanics of robot collectives from first principles.</p>
